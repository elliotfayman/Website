Electro Adhesion Cubesat

Created a C based Low pass filter and PID controller in order to precisely determine and control the orientation of a satellite.

Electro Adhesion Cubesat ADCS
The electro adhesion cube satellite is tiny satellite with the purpose of collecting small debris located in and around low earth orbit. This cube satellite received funding from NASA through the NASA Minds competition. The ADCS subsystem is a sub unit of the satellite responsible for detecting and controlling the orientation of the satellite. To determine the orientation of the satellite, an Arduino uno in combination with a mpu 6050 was used which possesses a gyroscope. To attenuate high frequency noise that is inherent with the mpu 6050's gyroscope, a C based butterworth low pass filter is used. To control the direction the satellite is pointed, a reaction wheel is used. The reaction wheel motor is a small brushless motor with a integrated halonomic sensor and speed controller. Precise speed control is achieved with a nested PID C based control loop: One coupled with the halonomic sensor to control the speed of the inertia wheel and another coupled with the mpu 6050 to control the rotational velocity of the entire cube satellite.

Metamorphic Virus Paper

Research paper investigating and eventually simulating the complex properties of metamorphic viruses using OOP Java.

Metamorphic Virus Paper
A metamorphic virus is a collection of malware with the capability to transform the code that generates the malware from generation to generation, as the malware propagates throughout the computer. In my research paper, I first identify the most common techniques by which these viruses transform themselves. I then simulate the virus and the host files using java and intentionally 'infect' the simulated files with a simulated metamorphic virus possessing one of the several metamorphic transformations I have identified. I then perform numerical analysis in order to determine the strengths and weaknesses of these transformations.

After School Robotics

Created and managed several after school robotics programs leveraging vex EDR and Lego Mindstorms kits to enhance STEM education across my community.

After School Robotics
As the Outreach Executive in my high school robotics team, I am responsible for leveraging the team's resources in order to generate interest and enthusiasm for STEM fields within my community. I employ 35 of the 42-student team as robotics coaches to participate in weekly nonmandatory but highly encouraged after school robotics programs in elementary and middle schools. At the start of each semester, I establish a general curriculum which conforms to each after school programs available resources and also caters to a variety of school grades from elementary school students to middle school students. Each Monday, I lead an outreach standup meeting amongst the participants establishing learning goals and helping to eliminate any blockers that our student coaches may have. Each week, I send the 35-person team of robotics coaches to 5 different schools (Tulsa Elementary, Balboa elementary, Topeka elementary, Castlebay Lane Charter, and Granada Hills TK-8 in Devonshire) ensuring that the students have proper transportation and teaching resources (either Lego Mindstorms kits or vex EDR robotics kits.) We have reached over 200 students through the outreach programs I helped establish.
