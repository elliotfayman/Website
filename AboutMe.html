Newly promoted Project manager of Matador Student Design (MSD) club at CSUN. Last year, I competed in the NASA minds competition from which it received funding for our proposed cube satellite debris collector. This satellite used electro adhesion in order to collect extremely small pieces of space debris. I was responsible for the research and design of our systems and controls subsystem for the cubesat. I developed a micro reaction wheel that helps the satellite detumble when it is deployed. This reaction wheel utilized an Arduino uno with a C implementation of a PID controller to help sense and adjust the orientation and rotational velocity of the cube satellite. Additionally, I helped develop a C program to help control the satellite throughout its life cycle from initial boot up, to detumbling, to error identification and assessment. Lastly, I managed a team of electrical engineers tasked with designing a PCB that could control the satellite.
